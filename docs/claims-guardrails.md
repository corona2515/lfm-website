## 2. Disallowed Claims (Updated)

### NEVER SAY - Absolute Prohibitions

#### Product History & Past Issues
| Forbidden | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "We rebuilt" / "We started over" | Implies previous product failed |
| "The old approach" / "Our previous version" | Acknowledges past problems |
| "We learned from our mistakes" | Admits fault |
| "Version 2.0" / "New and improved" | Implies old version was flawed |
| "After listening to feedback, we changed" | Sounds like damage control |
| "Teams said it was too much" | Criticizes our own past product |

#### Technology Terms - STRICTLY FORBIDDEN
| Term | Why |
|------|-----|
| "BIM" | Not part of current product positioning |
| "3D" | Not part of current product positioning |
| "Digital twin" | Not part of current product positioning |
| "Visualization" | Not part of current product positioning |
| "Patent" / "Patented" / "Patent-pending" | Cannot claim without verification |
| "Proprietary algorithm" | Vague and unverifiable |

#### Superlative & Hype Language
| Forbidden | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Industry-leading" | "Trusted by facilities teams" |
| "Best-in-class" | "Built for [specific outcome]" |
| "World's first" | [Don't replace - just remove] |
| "Revolutionary" | "A new approach" |
| "Game-changing" | "Significant improvement" |
| "Breakthrough" | "Effective" |
| "Cutting-edge" | "Modern" |
| "Next-generation" | [Just describe what it does] |
| "AI-powered" | "Prescriptiv analytics engine" |

### Guarantee Claims (Approved 2026-05-12)

LeanFM now offers a money-back ROI Savings Guarantee. The following marketing-surface language is approved:

| Approved Phrase | Where to Use |
|---|---|
| "3–5x ROI in energy savings" / "Backed by our money-back guarantee." | Homepage proof bar, segment pages |
| "Money-back ROI Guarantee" | Short standalone reference |
| "3x ROI or your money back." | Punchier callout variant |
| "Find 3x or more in savings — or we make it right." | Sales decks, longer-form variants |

**Prohibited variants:**
- Any guaranteed dollar amount per building (e.g., "We guarantee $50K+ savings per building")
- Any phrasing implying savings are automatic without the customer implementing the recommended corrective actions
- Inline "See terms" / asterisks / parenthetical hedges on marketing surfaces

The binding terms — mechanism, conditions, voiding events, claim process — live on `/terms` and are linked from the global site footer. They do not need to be repeated inline on marketing pages.

### 30% Energy Waste Claim (Reframed 2026-05-12)

The "30%" figure is retained but **reframed from a savings promise to a problem-size statement.**

**Approved framing (no disclaimer required):**
- "Hidden HVAC faults can quietly waste up to 30% of building energy spend."
- "Up to 30% of energy spend wasted by hidden HVAC faults."
- "Buildings with undetected faults can waste up to 30% of their energy budget."

**Prohibited variants (would require a disclaimer and conflict with the no-hedges rule):**
- "Save up to 30% on energy costs"
- "Reduce your energy bill by 30%"
- "30% savings guaranteed"
- Any phrasing that promises a specific percentage of savings to the customer

**Why this works:** The problem-size framing is an industry statement (well-established in AFDD literature and DOE/PNNL reports). It does not promise the customer they will save 30%. It frames the size of the prize, with the 3–5x ROI guarantee carrying the actual offer. No disclaimer needed because nothing is promised to a specific customer.

#### Accuracy Claims
| Forbidden | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "100% accurate" | Unverifiable |
| "No false positives" | Cannot guarantee |
| "Always catches" | Cannot guarantee |
| "Never misses" | Cannot guarantee |
| "Instant" results | Use onboarding-path language instead |
| "Automatic" fault detection | Use "automated" |
| "Machine learning" (without context) | Too vague |

#### Competitive Claims We Cannot Prove
| Forbidden | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| Naming specific competitors | Legal risk |
| "Market leader" | Unverified claim |
| "#1 solution" | Unverified claim |
| Pricing comparisons to named competitors | Cannot verify their pricing |
| Feature comparisons to named products | May be inaccurate |
| Customer count comparisons | Unverified |

#### Compliance & Certification Claims
| Forbidden | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| SOC 2 certified (unless verified) | Must have actual certification |
| HIPAA compliant (unless verified) | Must have actual compliance |
| FERPA compliant (unless verified) | Must have actual compliance |
| ISO certified (unless verified) | Must have actual certification |
| Any certification we don't hold | Legal liability |

### Phrases That Sound Like Admitting Fault
These phrases, even when meant positively, can imply past problems:

| Avoid | Sounds Like |
|-------|-------------|
| "Now you can finally..." | We couldn't do this before |
| "We've fixed the problem of..." | We had problems |
| "Unlike our previous approach..." | Our previous approach was wrong |
| "We've learned that..." | We didn't know before |
| "Based on customer feedback, we..." | Customers complained |
| "Simplified from..." | It was too complicated |
| "We listened and..." | We weren't listening before |
| "Better than ever" | It wasn't good before |

## 3. Required Disclaimers

### When Discussing Onboarding Timing
**Claims like:** "Guided onboarding while we scale" / "Timing varies by data quality and onboarding volume"

**Required Disclaimer (when asked for specifics):**
> "Processing and review timing varies based on dataset size, data quality, and onboarding volume."

**Placement Rules:**
- Tooltip or footer acceptable
- FAQ page can serve as central disclaimer
- Not required on every instance if general disclaimer exists

### For Testimonials & Quotes
**When using customer quotes:**
> "[Customer Name], [Title], [Organization]" - Must have written permission on file

**When using anonymized quotes:**
> "- Facilities Director, K-12 District" - Must indicate anonymized status internally

**For placeholder testimonials during development:**
> [PLACEHOLDER - Awaiting customer permission]

**Rules:**
- Never fabricate quotes
- Never attribute quotes to fictional people
- Never imply endorsement without explicit permission
- Keep permission documentation on file

### For Industry-Specific Claims
**K-12 Schools:**
When discussing student impact or learning environments:
> "Environmental improvements support but do not guarantee academic outcomes."

**Museums:**
When discussing collection protection:
> "Climate control recommendations support conservation goals. Always consult with conservation professionals for collection-specific requirements."

**Higher Education:**
When discussing campus-wide deployment:
> "Results based on individual building analysis. Campus-wide outcomes depend on portfolio characteristics."

### For Pricing
**While pricing is being finalized:**
> "Pricing shown is illustrative. Contact us for current rates."

**Once pricing is live:**
> "Prices subject to change. See terms for details."

### For Security Claims
**When discussing data security:**
> "See our Security page for complete details on our data protection practices."

## 4. Voice & Tone Guidelines for Building Owners

### Writing for Non-Technical Executives

**Do:**
- Lead with outcomes, not features
- Use financial language (ROI, savings, budget, costs)
- Speak to their goals (efficiency, sustainability, compliance)
- Respect their time - be concise
- Use confident, direct statements

**Don't:**
- Lead with technical specifications
- Assume they know HVAC terminology
- Use jargon without explanation
- Bury the value proposition
- Sound salesy or desperate

### ROI-Focused Language Patterns

**Instead of This:** | **Write This:**
--------------------|----------------
"Our AI analyzes your data" | "Find the faults draining your budget"
"Advanced fault detection algorithms" | "See exactly where you're losing money"
"Automated diagnostic capabilities" | "Know what to fix first for maximum impact"
"Cloud-based analytics platform" | "Get answers without IT involvement"
"Machine learning engine" | "Prescriptiv AI identifies your biggest opportunities"

### Confidence Without Arrogance

**Confident (Good):**
- "OnPoint finds faults other methods miss"
- "Our customers receive prioritized findings with clear next steps"
- "Built specifically for facilities teams"
- "We focus on what matters: getting you answers"

**Arrogant (Avoid):**
- "The only solution that actually works"
- "Everyone else is doing it wrong"
- "Obviously the best choice"
- "You'd be foolish not to..."

**Weak (Avoid):**
- "We think we can help"
- "Hopefully you'll see results"
- "We try to find faults"
- "Our solution might work for you"

### Tone Calibration by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Homepage hero | Bold, outcome-focused, confident |
| Feature pages | Clear, specific, benefit-driven |
| Pricing page | Transparent, value-focused, straightforward |
| Case studies | Evidence-based, specific, credible |
| Industry pages | Knowledgeable, empathetic, specialized |
| Support/FAQ | Helpful, clear, patient |

### Executive Summary Style
When writing for building owners and investors, structure content as:

1. **The Problem** (their pain point, in their language)
2. **The Cost** (what it's costing them now)
3. **The Solution** (what OnPoint does - one sentence)
4. **The Outcome** (specific benefit they care about)
5. **The Proof** (credibility marker)
6. **The Action** (clear next step)

## 5. Industry-Specific Guardrails

### K-12 Schools

**What We CAN Claim:**
- Energy savings potential (with standard disclaimers)
- Improved comfort in classrooms
- Reduced reactive maintenance
- Support for sustainability initiatives
- Budget optimization for facilities teams
- Compatibility with existing BAS systems

**What We CANNOT Claim:**
- Impact on student academic performance
- Teacher satisfaction improvements
- FERPA compliance (unless verified)
- Specific district cost savings without permission
- Safety improvements (beyond HVAC function)

**Compliance Considerations:**
- Student data is never collected by OnPoint
- Building data only - no personally identifiable information
- Procurement may require specific certifications - be accurate
- Some districts require on-premise options - don't promise if unavailable

**Sensitivity Notes:**
- School budgets are politically sensitive - don't imply waste
- Acknowledge the difficulty of facilities work in schools
- Respect that staff are often stretched thin
- Understand that decisions involve multiple stakeholders
- Avoid language that sounds like criticism of current staff

**Approved Messaging Angle:**
"Help your facilities team do more with the resources they have."

---

### Museums

**What We CAN Claim:**
- Support for environmental monitoring
- Identification of HVAC issues affecting climate control
- Energy efficiency improvements
- Operational cost reduction
- Faster response to environmental deviations

**What We CANNOT Claim:**
- Collection preservation guarantees
- Specific humidity/temperature accuracy levels
- Conservation-grade monitoring
- Art damage prevention
- Insurance premium reductions

**Compliance Considerations:**
- Museums may have specific environmental standards (AAM, etc.)
- Climate requirements vary dramatically by collection type
- Some museums have legacy systems with unique integration needs
- International museums may have different regulatory requirements

**Sensitivity Notes:**
- Collections are irreplaceable - never minimize this
- Conservation is a specialized profession - respect it
- Environmental control is mission-critical, not just cost savings
- Many museums are nonprofits with constrained budgets
- Board/donor dynamics can affect purchasing decisions

**Approved Messaging Angle:**
"Protect what matters most while managing operational realities."

---

### Higher Education

**What We CAN Claim:**
- Campus-wide portfolio visibility
- Energy reduction potential
- Support for sustainability commitments
- Reduced deferred maintenance backlog impact
- Operational efficiency improvements
- Compatibility with diverse building types

**What We CANNOT Claim:**
- Research facility suitability (without verification)
- Lab environment precision
- FERPA compliance (unless verified)
- Specific university cost savings without permission
- Impact on campus sustainability rankings

**Compliance Considerations:**
- Research facilities may have special requirements
- Some buildings may have federal funding compliance needs
- Procurement processes vary widely by institution
- IT security requirements may be stringent

**Sensitivity Notes:**
- Universities have complex governance structures
- Sustainability commitments are often public and tracked
- Facilities teams manage extremely diverse building portfolios
- Budget pressures are significant in higher ed
- Student and faculty comfort affects institutional reputation

**Approved Messaging Angle:**
"Meet your sustainability commitments while managing operational complexity."

## Quick Reference Card

### The 5-Second Check
Before any copy goes live, ask:

1. **Is it allowed?** (Check Section 1)
2. **Is it forbidden?** (Check Section 2)
3. **Does it need a disclaimer?** (Check Section 3)
4. **Is it right for our audience?** (Check Section 4)
5. **Is it appropriate for this industry?** (Check Section 5)

### Red Flag Words - Stop and Check
If you see these words, verify they're used correctly:

| Word | Action |
|------|--------|
| "Save" | Needs disclaimer if specific percentage |
| "Guarantee" | Probably not allowed |
| "Always" / "Never" | Probably not allowed |
| "Best" / "Leading" | Probably not allowed |
| "Patent" | Not allowed |
| "BIM" / "3D" | Not allowed |
| "Rebuilt" / "Previous" | Not allowed |
| "AI" (generic) | Use "Prescriptiv AI" instead |

### The Golden Rules

**Rule 1: If you can't prove it, don't claim it.**

**Rule 2: If you can prove it, say it simply.**

**Rule 3: Write for the building owner, not the engineer.**

**Rule 4: When in doubt, leave it out.**
